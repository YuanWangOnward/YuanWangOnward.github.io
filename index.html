Biophysically interpretable neural network design

Classical neural network focuses on performance of specific tasks, such as classification, tracking, considering little about internal meaning. We use neural network as a tool to understand the causal architecture in the brain. We design neural network deep down based on previous biophysical model, Dynamic Causal Modeling (DCM), proposing a generalized RNN (GRNN) and show that DCM can be cast as a specific GRNN. The new DCM-RNN extends DCM with a more flexible framework supporting more biophysically plausible modifications, new estimation methods, and neural network compatibility. In functional MRI (fMRI) experiments, DCM-RNN is shown to achieve more accurate causal architecture estimation and has more capacity of discovering simpler models among all the candidates than the classical DCM.

A new fixed-dictionary baseline for compressive sensing MRI reconstruction

People’s opinions about algorithms may be biased by incomplete comparison. Recently, researchers try to incorporate adaptive dictionaries into MRI Compressive Sensing (CS) algorithms to enhance MRI reconstruction and they compare their proposals with other adaptive dictionary algorithms. DLMRI is used as the baseline and up to 9dB image PSNR enhancement is often reported, which makes dictionary learning seem an extreme appealing research field. However, no comparison with a fixed-dictionary algorithm has been made which prevents a fair evaluation of the role of adaptive dictionaries in the first place. Herein, we propose a Fixed-Dictionary Baseline and compare it with the current baseline, DLMRI, and state-of-the-art algorithms, UNITE-MRI and DDTF-MRI on the same data set. Our study reveals that, the previous baseline DLMRI appears very week, even inferior to the simpler FDB algorithm. Reporting marked improvement over DLMRI can thus just be misleading.

Neural painting

It's a course project I advised. Two brilliant and passionated master students at NYU, Megan Hardy and Sumanto Pal, start their adventure of image processing and deep learning. Different objects of a painting typically have distinctively different styles i.e. textures and color compositions while previous neural painting works abstract a global style from a painting and apply it globally onto a photo. In this project, we separate objects of interest in both painting and real content photo and apply an art style only to the corresponding objects. For example, abstract style from a flower in a painting and apply it onto a flower in a real content photo. It creates unique aesthetic experiences.
